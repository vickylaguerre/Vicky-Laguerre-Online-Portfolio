Puppet Theater

Northwestern University - Segal Design Institute

Project Overview

As a Manufacturing and Design Intern at Northwestern University's Segal Design Institute, I had the opportunity to work on an exciting project to create an interactive puppet theater and play experience for children who frequently visit their loved ones in the Cook County Department of Corrections. This project aimed to foster imagination, creativity, and social development in these children, as well as promote a more positive environment and relationship between them and their loved ones with whom they only have limited time to see.

The puppet theater

Brainstorming Session

During our brainstorming session, my team members and I developed a multitude of ideas to meet the user needs and requirements for the puppet theater, but also thought of ways to make it more than a puppet theater, but an interactive play experience.

Team sketches

Final Concept Development

To accomplish the aforementioned project goals, we decided to create a modular, interactive play structure with the pupput theater as its focal point. This consisted of six distinct modules with different toys and interactive play elements for children, one of the modules being the puppet theater. The modules can be linked and oriented various ways, such as a straight line or a zig-zag. These modular features and interactive elements allow full autonomy for the children when playing with it, making it fun and engaging for the kids and their loved ones.

Final concept sketch

CAD Design & Rendering

The project demanded a meticulous approach to design, combining both digital and traditional manufacturing methods. Using computer-aided design software, I created detailed 3D models of every component, ensuring precise measurements and optimal assembly configuration. This digital approach allowed us to visualize the entire structure before production and make critical adjustments to improve stability and functionality. Special attention was given to designing child-safe joints and connections, leading to innovative solutions like finger joints and dowel assemblies that eliminated the need for metal fasteners.

CAD model

Manufacturing & Assembly

The manufacturing phase brought our digital designs to life through a combination of computer-aided manufacturing and traditional woodworking techniques. Each component was carefully cut and shaped using CNC machinery, ensuring consistency and precision across all parts. The assembly process required careful attention to detail, particularly in the joining of components where we utilized wood glue and dowels to create strong, permanent connections. This hands-on phase allowed us to refine our approach in real-time, making subtle adjustments to improve the structure's stability and ease of use. Throughout the process, we maintained a strong focus on safety and durability, ensuring that the final product would withstand regular use while remaining completely safe for children.

Assembling the modules

Project Outcome

The completed puppet theater project succeeded in creating a meaningful impact for children visiting the Cook County Department of Corrections. Beyond just a simple play structure, we delivered a versatile, six-module interactive system that serves as a bridge for connection between children and their loved ones. The modular design allows for multiple configuration options, keeping the experience fresh and engaging with each visit. The sturdy construction, child-safe materials, and thoughtful integration of various play elements have made it a valuable addition to the facility's visiting area. Most importantly, the theater has achieved its primary goal of fostering positive interactions and creating memorable experiences for families during their time together, helping to maintain and strengthen these important relationships through creative play and storytelling.

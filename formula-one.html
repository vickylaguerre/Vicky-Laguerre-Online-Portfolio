Differential Sprocket Adapter

Northwestern University Formula One Racing

Project Overview

As a member of the Northwestern University Formula One Racing team, specifically on the powertrain subteam, I was tasked with designing and manufacturing the differential sprocket adapter. This critical component connects the drive sprocket to the differential, effectively transferring power from the engine through the chain drive system to the wheels. The adapter needed to be precisely engineered to handle high torque loads while maintaining perfect alignment with both the sprocket and differential to ensure optimal performance during racing conditions.

Differential Sprocket Adapter(DSA)

Design Process

The design of the differential sprocket adapter was a critical component of our electric vehicle. Using Solidworks CAD, I created a detailed 3D model of the adapter, ensuring precise measurements and compatibility with existing components.

CAD model of the Differential Sprocket Adapter

Analysis & Optimization

To ensure the durability and performance of the adapter, I conducted Finite Element Analysis (FEA). This process allowed us to identify potential stress points and optimize the design for maximum efficiency and reliability.

FEA results for the DSA

Manufacturing

Following the design and analysis phases, I oversaw the manufacturing process of the sprocket adapter by using Fusion 360 computer-aided manufacturing to load the CAD file of the part onto the CNC for it to be machined. This hands-on experience provided valuable insights into translating digital designs into physical components.

Manufacturing the DSA

Implementation & Project Outcome

  • Performed meticulous post-processing, including deburring and surface finishing, to enhance the adapter's fit, functionality, and longevity.
  • Determined appropriate fasteners and fastening techniques to ensure secure and reliable attachment of the adapter to the powertrain components.
  • Resolved alignment and fitment challenges during installation, ensuring seamless integration with the vehicle.
  • Delivered a robust, functional component that contributed to the overall reliability and performance of the racing car.
